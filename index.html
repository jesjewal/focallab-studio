About the math
Thin-lens model. Circle of confusion c = active-area diagonal ÷ 1500. Hyperfocal H = f²/(N·c) + f. Near limit s(H−f)/(H+s−2f), far limit s(H−f)/(H−s) (∞ when s ≥ H). Background blur disc for a point at distance x: b = (f²/N)·(x−s)/(x·(s−f)), shown as a fraction of frame height — that is the size of the bokeh balls you see. The preview is physically scaled: subject size comes from the real field of view at the focus distance, and every light is drawn at its computed blur-disc diameter. Cinema aspect ratios are treated as a sensor-height crop; T-stops are used as the f-number for depth purposes. Lighting uses inverse-square falloff (I ∝ 1/d²) — note how on-camera flash barely reaches a distant background: relative exposure falls off as (s/x)². Lens character (cat-eye, swirl, onion rings) is not simulated.
